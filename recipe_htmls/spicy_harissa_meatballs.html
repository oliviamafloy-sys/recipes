🥩 Beef

Spicy Harissa Meatballs

↗ View original recipe
Servings
4
Time
~45 min
Main Ingredients
Beef mince, harissa, cherry tomatoes, cream
Calories
~495 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Meatballs
  • 500g beef mince
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt & pepper
Sauce
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, diced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 350g cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red pepper, sliced
  • 2 tbsp harissa paste
  • 200ml thickened cream
  • Splash of water
  • Oil & salt
To Finish
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • Chilli flakes & coriander, to serve
🍳 Recipe
  1. In a large bowl, mix the mince with all the spices and egg until well combined. Roll into small balls. If you have time, leave them to rest for a while to marinade.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan and fry the meatballs for a few minutes on all sides to sear. Remove from the pan and drain any excess oil, leaving a small amount for the veg.
  3. In the same pan, add the red onion and a pinch of salt. Cook for a few minutes, then add a splash of water to deglaze the pan. Add the garlic and tomato paste and cook for a couple more minutes.
  4. Add the cherry tomatoes and red pepper, place a lid on the pan and cook for 15 minutes until everything has broken down.
  5. Blend until smooth, then stir in the harissa paste and double cream. Return the meatballs to the sauce, cover and cook for a further 5–10 minutes.
  6. In the last few minutes, add the spinach and allow to wilt. Serve topped with chilli flakes and coriander.

Notes

  • Lamb mince works as an alternative if you prefer a richer flavour.
  • Add an extra tbsp of harissa if you like more heat.
  • Great served over rice, couscous or with crusty bread to mop up the sauce.
  • Leftovers keep well for 3 days and the flavour deepens overnight.
