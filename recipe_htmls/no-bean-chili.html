🥩 Beef

No Bean Chili

↗ View original recipe
Servings
6
Time
40 mins
Main Ingredients
Ground Beef, Diced Tomatoes
Calories
~420 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Chili
  • 2 lbs (907g) ground beef
  • 3 × 14.5 oz cans diced tomatoes
  • 1 × 15 oz can tomato sauce
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1¼ cups beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large sweet onion, chopped
Chili Seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons cumin
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1¼ teaspoons coarse salt, to taste
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
To Serve (optional)
  • Fresh parsley
  • Chopped green onions
  • Shredded cheese
  • Greek yogurt or sour cream
🍳 Recipe
  1. In a 5- or 6-quart Dutch oven or large pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 2 minutes, until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add the ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook for 5–6 minutes until browned, then drain off any excess grease.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and all chili seasoning, mixing well to coat the beef evenly.
  4. Pour in the beef broth, canned diced tomatoes (with their liquid), and tomato sauce. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer for 30–60 minutes, stirring occasionally — the longer it simmers, the deeper the flavour.
  6. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed. Ladle into bowls and top with your favourite garnishes.

Notes

  • Spicy version: sauté ¼–⅓ cup chopped pickled jalapeños with the onion, then add 3 tbsp Frank's Hot Sauce with the broth, plus ¼ tsp cayenne and 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes with the other seasonings.
  • Make ahead: Stores well in the fridge for 2–3 days. Reheat on the stovetop; stir in an extra tablespoon of tomato paste if you'd like it thicker.
  • Consistency: For a soupier chili, add an extra splash of beef broth before serving.
