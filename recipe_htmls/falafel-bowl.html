Falafel Bowl

↗ Seko Foods Brisbane
Servings
2
Time
30 mins
Main Ingredients
Seko Falafels, Ajvar, Tzatziki
Calories
~595 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Falafels
  • 6 Seko Foods frozen falafels (3 per serve)
Buttered Stock Rice
  • 60g jasmine rice (uncooked, yields ~75g cooked per serve)
  • 150ml chicken or vegetable stock
  • 10g butter
Red Cabbage Slaw
  • 100g red cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 50g red onion, thinly sliced
  • 60g carrot, grated
Lemon Vinaigrette
  • 30ml lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
  • 20ml apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 10ml olive oil
Tzatziki
  • 100g Woolworths light Greek yogurt
  • 60g cucumber, grated and squeezed dry
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 20ml lemon juice
  • Fresh dill (optional)
To Serve
  • 60g I Love Ajvar paprika relish (2 tbsp per serve)
🍳 Recipe
  1. Rinse the jasmine rice and add to a small saucepan with the stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 12 minutes until the liquid is fully absorbed. Remove from heat, stir in the butter, and leave covered for 5 minutes.
  2. While the rice cooks, heat the falafels: air fry at 160°C for 7 minutes, or oven bake at 180°C for 5 minutes until warmed through and lightly crispy. Do not heat from frozen — thaw first if needed.
  3. Make the tzatziki: grate the cucumber and squeeze firmly in a clean cloth or paper towel to remove excess liquid. Combine with the light Greek yogurt, garlic, lemon juice, and dill if using. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Combine the red cabbage, red onion, and grated carrot in a bowl. Whisk together the lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, then toss the dressing through the slaw just before serving.
  5. Divide the buttered rice between two bowls. Add 3 falafels per bowl alongside a portion of slaw, a generous spoonful of ajvar, and a dollop of tzatziki.

Notes

  • Seko Foods falafels are made in Brisbane and available at sekofood.com.au. Per packet: do not microwave from frozen — thaw first or heat in oven/air fryer directly from fridge.
  • Dress the slaw just before serving to keep it crisp. If making ahead, store the dressing separately.
  • Use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock to keep this fully vegetarian.
  • The lemon vinaigrette is very low in calories (~50 kcal per serve) — a good swap for any creamy dressing.
