Bang Bang Shrimp

↗ View original recipe
Servings
2
Time
~40 min
Main Ingredients
Prawns, cornstarch, bang bang sauce
Calories
~435 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Prawns
  • 265g large prawns, peeled & deveined
Wet Batter
  • ⅔ cup buttermilk
  • ⅓ tbsp sriracha
Dry Batter
  • ⅔ cup cornstarch
  • 1½ tsp garlic powder
  • ⅔ tsp onion powder
  • ⅓ tsp MSG or salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying
Bang Bang Sauce
  • ⅓ cup mayo
  • 3 tbsp Thai sweet chilli sauce
  • 2½ tbsp sriracha
  • ⅔ tbsp lemon juice
To Serve
  • Steamed rice
  • Chives or spring onion
  • Chilli oil drizzle
🍳 Recipe
  1. Combine the buttermilk and sriracha in a bowl. Add the prawns, toss to coat and leave to soak for 20 minutes.
  2. Mix together the bang bang sauce ingredients in a separate bowl and set aside.
  3. Combine the cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder and salt in a shallow bowl for the dry batter.
  4. Remove prawns from the buttermilk and shake off excess. Coat in the cornstarch mixture and shake off any excess.
  5. Heat oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Fry the prawns for about 4 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Drain and allow to cool slightly.
  6. Toss the fried prawns in the bang bang sauce. Serve over steamed rice garnished with chives and a drizzle of chilli oil.

Notes

  • Don't skip the 20 minute buttermilk soak — it keeps the prawns tender inside.
  • Shake off excess batter well or it won't crisp up properly.
  • For a lighter version, air fry at 200°C for 8–10 minutes instead of deep frying.
  • The bang bang sauce makes more than needed — great as a dipping sauce or on other dishes.
