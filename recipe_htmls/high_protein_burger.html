🥩 Beef

High Protein Burger

↗ View original recipe
Servings
1
Time
~15 min
Main Ingredients
Lean beef mince, light cheese, burger bun
Calories
514 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Burger
  • 166g extra lean beef mince (makes 2 patties)
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 burger bun of choice
  • 2 light cheese slices
  • Lettuce, tomato, pickles, onion
High Protein Burger Sauce
  • 100g fat-free Greek yoghurt
  • 70g fat-free mayo
  • 15g reduced-sugar ketchup
  • 10g mustard
  • 10g pickles, finely chopped
  • 10g pickle juice
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt to taste
🍳 Recipe
  1. Mix all burger sauce ingredients together and refrigerate until ready to serve. This can be made in advance and keeps for several days.
  2. Divide the mince into two equal portions and press into thin patties. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat a pan or griddle over high heat. Cook the patties for 2–3 minutes per side until a deep crust forms. Add a cheese slice on top of each patty in the last minute and cover briefly to melt.
  4. Toast the burger bun if desired. Stack the patties with lettuce, tomato, onion and pickles. Spoon over the burger sauce and serve immediately.

Notes

  • The sauce makes more than one serving — store in the fridge for up to 5 days.
  • Extra lean mince keeps calories low but can dry out — don't overcook.
  • A smash burger technique (pressing flat on a hot pan) gives the best crust.
  • Scale up the patties easily — the sauce ratio works for multiple burgers.
