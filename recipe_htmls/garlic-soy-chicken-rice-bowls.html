Garlic Soy Chicken Rice Bowls with Chili Sesame Sauce

↗ View original recipe
Servings
2
Time
~35 mins + marinate
Main Ingredients
Chicken, Rice, Sesame
Calories
~620 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Chicken & Marinade
  • 225g boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • ½ tbsp rice vinegar
  • ½ tbsp sesame oil
  • ½ tsp grated fresh ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Sesame Rice
  • 1 cup cooked jasmine rice
  • ½ tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp green onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
Chili Sesame Sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • ½ tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tbsp chili paste (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp honey
  • ½ tsp rice vinegar
  • ½ tsp toasted sesame seeds
Toppings
  • Sliced cucumber
  • Extra chopped green onions
  • Chili flakes (optional)
🍳 Recipe
  1. In a bowl, whisk together minced garlic, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, grated ginger, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken thighs and toss to coat well. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours in the fridge for deeper flavour.
  2. Preheat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Remove chicken from the marinade and cook for 6–7 minutes per side until cooked through and nicely caramelised (internal temp 75°C / 165°F). Let rest for a few minutes, then slice into strips.
  3. While the chicken rests, combine the cooked jasmine rice with sesame oil, chopped green onions, and cilantro. Mix well to coat the rice evenly.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together all chili sesame sauce ingredients — sesame oil, soy sauce, chili paste, honey, rice vinegar, and toasted sesame seeds — until well combined.
  5. Divide the sesame rice between two bowls. Top with sliced chicken and cucumber slices. Drizzle generously with chili sesame sauce and garnish with extra green onions and chili flakes if desired. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Marinating overnight gives the best depth of flavour — prep the night before if you can.
  • Chicken thighs stay juicier than breast for this recipe — don't substitute unless needed.
  • The chili sesame sauce keeps in the fridge for up to a week — make a double batch.
  • Add sliced avocado, edamame, or shredded carrots for extra colour and nutrition.
  • Use tamari instead of soy sauce to make this gluten-free.
