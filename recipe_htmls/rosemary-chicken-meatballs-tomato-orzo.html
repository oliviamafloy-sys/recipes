Rosemary Chicken Meatballs & Tomato Orzo

↗ View original recipe
Servings
2
Time
~45 mins
Main Ingredients
Chicken, Orzo, Tomato
Calories
~680 kcal / serve
🥬 Ingredients
Meatballs
  • 250g ground chicken
  • 2½ tbsp panko breadcrumbs
  • 1½ tbsp grated Parmesan
  • 1 small egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • ⅓ tsp salt
  • Pinch black pepper
  • Pinch red pepper flakes (optional)
Tomato Orzo
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • ⅓ small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 155g (½ can) crushed tomatoes
  • 85g (⅓ can) tomato sauce/passata
  • ⅓ cup (60g) dry orzo pasta
  • ⅔ cup chicken broth
  • ⅓ tsp dried oregano
  • Pinch dried basil
  • Pinch red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
To Serve
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra grated Parmesan
🍳 Recipe
  1. In a large bowl, gently combine all meatball ingredients — ground chicken, panko, Parmesan, egg, garlic, rosemary, olive oil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Mix until just combined; don't overwork. Form into small meatballs, about 2–3cm in diameter (you should get roughly 8–10). Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Optional but recommended: cover meatballs with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes to help them hold their shape.
  3. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Bake meatballs for 20–25 minutes until lightly golden and cooked through (internal temp 74°C / 165°F). Alternatively, pan-fry in olive oil over medium heat for 8–10 minutes, turning to brown all sides.
  4. While meatballs cook, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 5–7 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Add dry orzo and chicken broth. Stir to combine, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until orzo is cooked and liquid is mostly absorbed. Add a splash more broth if needed.
  7. Gently stir the cooked meatballs into the tomato orzo. Simmer together for 5 minutes to meld the flavours. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh basil and extra grated Parmesan.

Notes

  • Don't overmix the meatball mixture — it keeps them tender, not tough.
  • Chilling the meatballs before cooking is optional but helps them hold their shape, especially if pan-frying.
  • If the orzo absorbs liquid too fast, add a splash of extra chicken broth.
  • Leftovers keep for up to 3 days in the fridge — add a little broth when reheating as the orzo thickens.
  • Swap ground chicken for ground turkey if preferred.
